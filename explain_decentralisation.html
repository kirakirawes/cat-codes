Explaining Decentralisation to Bean

"What is decentralisation? - You asked.
Today, I am going to explain decentralisation to you.

Dear Bean, 'decentralisation' may sound like a big and difficult word, but trust me, it shouldn't be. 

The governance of the world that we live in today is very centralised. Centralisation is the concentration of control of an activity or organisation under a single authority. Imagine that in a group of five friends, there is only one who always tells the other four what to do, how to do it, and when to do it. 

Well, decentralisation is the opposite of that. In a decentralised world, everybody can participate in the process of decision-making and designing the future in an equal way. Decentralisation comes with transparency, diversity, and harmony. It enables the coexistence of different voices from all the corners of the globe, no matter what skin colour your have or the cultural background where you come from. It brings about autonomy, resilience, and equity in our community. That sounds nice, doesn’t it?  

An example of decentralisation is our language. Languages are always decentralised, in a logical way. The way Vietnamese is spoken between you and me, is different from that between our parents. There is no centralised setting required for a language to exist, and its rules of grammar aren't created, used, and controlled by any one single person. The decentralisation of our language has contributed a great deal to its beauty and versatility. That’s why we have so many great pieces of literature to enjoy. 

You know I care about decentralisation a great deal. And as I am explaining decentralisation to you, I am still learning about it. It is a concept that is very important to me and my works at Decentralisation Off The Shelf. Like you, everyday I am trying my best to make sense of the world, and I still haven’t fully understood how the world-order has come to be. I only hope that through the lens of decentralisation I will know better, and I’d wish the same for you.
